# Cage for mounting monitors and cameras
To build the cage you will want to cut the thor labs [optical rails](https://www.thorlabs.com/thorproduct.cfm?partnumber=XE25RL2) down to size and then thread them with an imperial thread drill.
Here are the sizes that you will need to cut:

4 x 540mm Cage floor pieces
4 x 540mm Cage top pieces
4 x 600mm Cage vertical pieces
1 x 540mm cross bar for camera mounting


## making the monitor mounts
To mount the monitors you will need to 3D print:
8x top monitor mounts
8x bottom monitor mounts
16x right angled brackets (XE25A90) - These can also be 3D printed from the Thor labs cad file, but i recommend having 8 real metal pieces for the bottom monitor mounts since these will bear the weight of the monitors

Attach the 3D printed parts onto the right angled brackets, these should just slot in to the right angled brackets by placing the cylindrical part of the 3D printed pieces into the circular whole on the right angled bracket. Then slot these onto the monitors with the bottom pieces attaching to the bottom of the monitor and the top pieces on the top of the monitor.

# Building the cage
Attach the 4 x Cage floor pieces together in a square using the corner cubes and screw them into place. Then add the vertical cage pieces to these cubes such that they form the side breams of the cage, projecting upwards.

Once this structure is made you can then attach the monitors to the cage, with each monitor sitting between the vertical cage pieces. The right angled brackets of the monitor mounts can then be fixed onto the rails of these side pieces using the [Drop-In T-Nut, 1/4\"-20 Tapped Hole, 10 Pack](http://www.thorlabs.com/newgrouppage9.cfm?objectgroup_id=4101) and screwed into place.

Once these monitors are in place add the top cage pieces above the monitors so that they form beams between each of the vertical cage pieces, creating a cube. The cross bar for mounting can then be added by fixing it onto the top of the cube with right angled brackets. 

# Mounting the camera to cross beam
The first camera can be mounted to a right angled bracket attached to the cross beam such that it points directly downwards, giving a birds eye view of the cage. A second camera can be added to one of the top cage pieces at an angle, this will allow for the mouse's pose to be reconstructed in 3D by DLClive.
